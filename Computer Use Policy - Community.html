Community Computer Use Policy

In order to enhance and support its mission to provide information resources and research assistance in pursuit of scholarly and creative endeavors, Huie Library provides computers and internet access for Henderson State University students, faculty, staff, and community library cardholders.

Community Computer Use

As a service to the community, two computers, located near the Circulation Desk, are provided for community use with the following guidelines:

  • In order to use the community computers, a patron must have a valid Huie Library Community card. For instructions on obtaining a card, please see our Community Patron Policies.
  • Before their first session, community patrons must register at the Circulation desk to obtain a username.
  • Community patrons are limited to two hours of computer use per day.
  • If all computers are in use, a community patron seeking to use a computer may ask to be put on a waiting list for the next available computer.
  • Community computers are available on a first-come, first-served basis and cannot be reserved for specific times.
  • Community computers are available from the opening of the library until ten minutes prior closing.
  • Printing is available from the community computers for 5 cents per page. Please pick up and pay for prints at the Circulation Desk.
  • At a librarian's discretion, a community patron pursuing coursework may be logged onto a Student computer.

Misuse

Access to the computers and the internet is a privilege and not a right. Misuse of computers will result in revocation of internet and computer privileges. Misuse includes but is not limited to:

  • Attempting to override computer system security
  • Destruction of or damage to equipment, data, or software
  • Disruption or unauthorized monitoring of electronic communication
  • Harassment of others
  • Reconfiguring workstations
  • Signing into more than one computer at a time
  • Signing others, including family members, onto computers
  • Use of accounts or network identification assigned to others
  • Violation of another's privacy
  • Violation of software license agreements

Content

Huie Library does not censor its resources. The library is responsible only for the content of those pages bearing its name. The library is not responsible for the accuracy of information found on the internet. Patrons should evaluate sites for content and accuracy.

Note: In compliance with Arkansas state law, Community Patrons under the age of 18 cannot use Huie Library's computers.

This policy is subject to change without notice.
